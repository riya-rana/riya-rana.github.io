Intellectual Property Landscaping: Service Design

INTRODUCTION

During an Internship at Tata Innovation Labs (Mumbai, 2014), I performed Intellectual Property Landscaping using open source data wherein I analyzed the patent situation across the area of service design. I created a combined, detailed IP analysis information and presented it in a defined format such as a landscape. This analysis aids in figuring out the direction in which the technology is advancing. In- and out-licensing opportunities, competitor strengths or weaknesses, Freedom to operate issues can be identified and explored.

New junctions for research and development in Service Design can be identified and analyzed, also called as ‘white gaps’ or ‘scope of research’. Finding this scope of research allows one to further:

  • Construct new models, theories, frameworks to develop pattern language dialects of Service Synthesis
  • Create artifacts such as Service Experience Design Frameworks for wide deployment
  • Eventually, develop a Software Tool as a Prototype


FLOW OF WORK

I used Google.com/patents as my main source. I focused on the abstract part of service as that was the requirement. Any patents related to the program, telecommunications aspects were not considered. Through exploration of various patents, I identified a list of queries which were the most relevant to the project. Examples queries:

  • Service Design Pattern, Model, Innovation, Engineering
  • Service Management
  • Business Service & Engineering
  • Service Industries/Organization, Environment
  • Service Evaluation
  • Service Process
  • Service Science Management and Engineering

The search query would give me certain relevant patents, whose Inventor’s other patents I could further look up and thus creating a cycle of information gathering

  • Patent → Inventor → Patents...
  • Patent → Citations → Patents → Inventors → Patents...

A large database of patents and their details was created in Excel. This data was then analyzed for main query categories, branches, connections between authors and patents, companies and patents, and visually represented using Visio. I created a more interactive, clickable version using a Mind Map.
